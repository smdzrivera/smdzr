Reading reflections week 1

I really liked the idea from Rules of Play about video games being unproductive, in the sense that they don’t generate profit or material wealth for the player. Instead, they create a space for experiences that exist outside of economic gain -—time reserved purely for play, exploration, and imagination. This idea of unproductiveness feels meaningful because it values the experience itself, not what it can produce or achieve. It reminds me of the Account of the Peach Blossom Spring, where the fisherman finds a hidden utopia but can never return to it. Like video games, the Peach Blossom Spring isn’t about owning or gaining something tangible; it’s about the fleeting, transformative experience it offers. Even though neither the game nor the utopia can be fully captured or held onto, they leave behind something powerful—a memory, a feeling, or an inspiration that stays with you. Both show how some of the most meaningful experiences in life are the ones that can’t be measured or commodified, but still move us in profound ways.
